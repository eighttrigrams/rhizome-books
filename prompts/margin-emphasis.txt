You are shown one or two tall, narrow strips cropped from the MARGINS of a
scanned book page (left and/or right), upscaled, each including a sliver of
the adjacent body text.

Your ONLY job: find passages SINGLED OUT by an emphatic MARGIN mark, and say
which body text they sit beside. Emphatic margin marks include:
- a bold / heavy stroke, or a boldly drawn hook or curved bracket
- an arrow or caret pointing at the text
- an exclamation "!" — a hand-drawn one is a bold vertical or slanted stroke,
  often with a dot, tick, or curl at its base (it will NOT look typeset)
- a star, a circle / loop drawn around words, or a written comment ("HA", "?")

IGNORE:
- thin, light, plain lines that merely run alongside a long paragraph
- underlines beneath the body text (those are handled elsewhere)
- printed text, page numbers, edge shadows, bleed-through

A mark that POINTS AT or boldly brackets a SHORT, specific passage is what you
want; a faint line spanning a whole paragraph is not.

For each singled-out spot, output one line, exactly:
SINGLED OUT: "<first ~6 words of the body text beside the mark>" — <the mark>

If there are none, output exactly: NONE
Output only those lines (or NONE). No preamble, no commentary.
